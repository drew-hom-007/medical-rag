Heart failure is a condition where the heart cannot pump enough blood to meet the body's needs.

Heart failure is classified as either reduced ejection fraction, where the heart muscle is weak, or preserved ejection fraction, where the heart muscle is stiff.

Common symptoms of heart failure include breathlessness, ankle swelling, fatigue, and reduced exercise tolerance.

The New York Heart Association classification grades heart failure severity from class 1 with no symptoms to class 4 with symptoms at rest.

ACE inhibitors are the cornerstone of heart failure treatment as they reduce the workload on the heart and improve survival.

Beta blockers such as bisoprolol and carvedilol are used in stable heart failure to reduce heart rate and improve cardiac function.

Diuretics such as furosemide relieve fluid overload symptoms like breathlessness and ankle swelling in heart failure patients.

SGLT-2 inhibitors have recently been shown to significantly reduce hospitalisation and death in heart failure patients.

Patients with heart failure should monitor their weight daily as sudden weight gain of more than 2kg in 2 days indicates fluid retention.

Cardiac resynchronisation therapy uses a specialised pacemaker to improve the coordination of heart contractions in selected heart failure patients.
