Hypertension is defined as persistently elevated blood pressure of 130/80 mmHg or higher according to current guidelines.

Blood pressure is measured in two numbers. Systolic pressure is the top number and measures pressure when the heart beats. Diastolic pressure is the bottom number and measures pressure between beats.

Primary hypertension has no identifiable cause and develops gradually over years. Secondary hypertension is caused by an underlying condition such as kidney disease or hormonal disorders.

Lifestyle modifications are the foundation of hypertension treatment and include reducing sodium intake, regular aerobic exercise, limiting alcohol, quitting smoking, and maintaining a healthy weight.

ACE inhibitors such as ramipril and lisinopril are commonly used first-line medications for hypertension. They are particularly beneficial in patients with diabetes or kidney disease.

Calcium channel blockers such as amlodipine are effective antihypertensive medications and are often preferred in older patients or those of African-Caribbean origin.

Thiazide diuretics help lower blood pressure by reducing fluid volume in the body. They are often used in combination with other antihypertensive agents.

Beta blockers such as bisoprolol are used in hypertension when there is also heart failure or a history of heart attack.

Resistant hypertension is defined as blood pressure that remains above target despite taking three or more antihypertensive medications at optimal doses.

Uncontrolled hypertension significantly increases the risk of stroke, heart attack, heart failure, kidney failure, and vision loss.
