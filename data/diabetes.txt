Type 2 diabetes is a chronic condition where the body becomes resistant to insulin or doesn't produce enough insulin to maintain normal blood sugar levels.

Common symptoms include increased thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision, and slow healing of cuts and bruises.

First-line treatment for type 2 diabetes is lifestyle modification including a healthy diet, regular physical activity, and weight loss if overweight.

Metformin is the most commonly prescribed first medication for type 2 diabetes. It works by reducing glucose production in the liver and improving insulin sensitivity.

Blood sugar monitoring is essential for diabetes management. Patients should aim for fasting blood glucose levels between 4 and 7 mmol/L.

HbA1c is a blood test that reflects average blood sugar levels over the past 3 months. A target HbA1c below 7% is recommended for most adults with type 2 diabetes.

Complications of uncontrolled diabetes include kidney disease, nerve damage, eye problems, and increased risk of heart disease and stroke.

SGLT-2 inhibitors such as empagliflozin and dapagliflozin are newer diabetes medications that also provide heart and kidney protection.

GLP-1 receptor agonists such as semaglutide help lower blood sugar, promote weight loss, and reduce cardiovascular risk in diabetic patients.

Regular screening for diabetes complications should include annual eye exams, kidney function tests, foot examinations, and cardiovascular risk assessment.
